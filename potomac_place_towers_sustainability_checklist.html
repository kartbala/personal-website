Potomac Place Towers Sustainability Checklist

Proposed sustainability measures for review by the Condo Sustainability Committee

Thermostat Alignment & Locks

  • Align thermostats with Washington, D.C. standards (78°F in summer, 68°F in winter).
  • Install thermostat locks in South Laundry Room, North Laundry Room, and Basement.
  • Estimated cost: ~$15 each.
  • Citation: DC DOEE – Energy Tips for Institutional Buildings

Separate Water Meters (Including Landscaping)

  • Install separate water meters for building and irrigation.
  • Benefit: avoids sewer charges on irrigation water.
  • Estimated cost: $1,200 – $2,700 (includes DC Water fees, plumber prep, backflow prevention).
  • Example savings: ~$322 per year if 20,000 gallons used for landscaping.
  • Citations:
    • DC Water – Meter Services
    • DC Water Sewer Rates FY2025

Motion Sensor Lighting

  • Verify all motion sensors are working.
  • Ensure all basement/common-area lighting is on motion sensors.
  • Potential savings: up to 40% reduction in lighting costs.
  • Citation: U.S. DOE – Occupancy Sensor Energy Savings Guide

Solar Roof Analysis

  • Conduct full solar feasibility analysis (structural + energy output).
  • Explore options for rooftop solar electricity or solar hot water.
  • Note incentives: DC Solar for All program, Renewable Energy Credits, federal tax credits.
  • Citations:
    • DC DOEE – Solar for All
    • U.S. DOE – Solar Energy Technologies Office

Next Steps

  • Gather final cost estimates.
  • Present to condo board for approval.
  • Phase implementation (low-cost measures first, capital projects after).

Prepared for Potomac Place Towers Condo Sustainability Committee, Washington, D.C.
