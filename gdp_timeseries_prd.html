GDP Time-Series Visualizer

1. Introduction

This document outlines a minimalist web tool for visualizing a country's historical Gross Domestic Product (GDP). The goal is to present complex economic data in a clean, highly legible format using a strictly black and white palette.

2. Vision and Opportunity

The visualizer transforms dense economic data into a simple narrative. By focusing on typography and negative space, it aims to make macroeconomic trends engaging for students, journalists, and curious readers alike.

3. Target Audience

  • Primary: Students, educators, and journalists.
  • Secondary: Business professionals, financial analysts, and researchers.
  • Tertiary: Anyone curious about global economics.

4. User Flow

  1. Landing page prompts the user with "TYPE A COUNTRY".
  2. After submission, the screen transitions to the GDP visualization.

5. Aesthetic & Design Requirements

The interface follows a strict black (#000000) background with white (#FFFFFF) typography and chart elements. The country name appears in a bold sans-serif display font such as Inter Bold or Helvetica Now Display. Axes and labels use a highly readable sans-serif like Inter or SF Pro Text. No gradients, shadows, or other ornamentation are used.

6. Functional Requirements

  • Smart country input that recognizes common aliases (e.g., "USA" or "United States").
  • Fetch historical GDP in current US dollars from a local database.
  • Render a vertical bar chart with an overlaid smoothed line graph, clearly labeling axes.
  • Minimal interactive controls: an information/source toggle and a shareable URL button.

7. Data Requirements

Data originates from the World Bank's GDP (current US$) dataset. The data is cleaned and stored locally for quick queries, covering available years from 1960 onward.

8. Minimum Viable Product

  • Input supporting at least 100 major countries and their aliases.
  • Database populated with World Bank GDP data.
  • Complete visualization in the high-legibility aesthetic.
  • Working info/source panel and shareable links.

9. Future Enhancements

  • Additional economic indicators such as GDP per capita and GDP growth.
  • Ability to compare multiple countries on one graph.
  • Logarithmic scale toggle.
  • Currency conversion options and data export functions.
